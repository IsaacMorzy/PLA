Most donors hesitate for one reason: uncertainty.
Not about generosity—about confidence.

Before you contribute to any campaign, use this simple framework to decide with clarity.

## 1) Check whether the need is specific
A credible campaign states:
- who needs help,
- what support is required,
- and why action is urgent now.

If the problem statement is vague, pause and gather more context.

## 2) Check progress against a clear goal
You should be able to verify:
- current amount raised,
- target amount,
- and remaining distance to goal.

Clear progress indicators help you understand whether your contribution can move the outcome.

## 3) Check whether campaign activity is visible
A trustworthy campaign should show signs of active support and updates.
Visible momentum reduces ambiguity and improves confidence before checkout.

## 4) Check whether the donation flow is clear
The best campaign pages make the next step obvious:
review → decide → donate.

If the action flow feels confusing, donors are more likely to abandon.

## 5) Make your decision and document your giving habit
After donating, save the campaign and revisit progress later.
Building a repeatable giving process helps you support more effectively over time.
